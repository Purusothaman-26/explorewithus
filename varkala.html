Varkala

About Varkala

Varkala, located in Kerala, is a scenic coastal town known for its stunning cliffs adjacent to the Arabian Sea. It is famous for its pristine beaches, laid-back vibe, and spiritual atmosphere.

A perfect retreat for beach lovers, wellness seekers, and spiritual travelers, Varkala blends natural beauty with a touch of culture and tranquility.

Visit Papanasam Beach to witness breathtaking sunsets and take a holy dip believed to wash away sins. Explore Janardanaswamy Temple, enjoy yoga and Ayurvedic massages, or relax in cafés along the cliff. Varkala is Kerala’s hidden seaside gem.

Top Travel Destinations in Varkala

  • Papanasam Beach – Sacred beach known for stunning sunsets
  • Varkala Cliff – Iconic cliff with cafés and ocean views
  • Janardanaswamy Temple – 2,000-year-old Hindu pilgrimage site
  • Sivagiri Mutt – Spiritual center founded by Sree Narayana Guru
  • Varkala Tunnel – Historic 19th-century canal system
  • Anchuthengu Fort – Coastal fort offering history and sea views
  • Black Sand Beach – Unique and quiet stretch for peaceful walks
  • Kappil Lake – Scenic lake for boating near backwaters

Travel Tips for Visiting Varkala

  • Best time to visit: November to March for pleasant weather.
  • Respect local customs: Cover up when near temples or local villages.
  • Try Ayurvedic treatments: Varkala is known for authentic wellness centers.
  • Stay on the cliff: Offers best views and access to beaches and cafés.
  • Carry swimwear: Ideal for enjoying the beaches and waves.
  • Sunset viewing: Don’t miss the view from the cliff at dusk.
  • Use local taxis/bikes: Easy to explore nearby beaches and points.
  • Book early: Peak season accommodations fill fast.

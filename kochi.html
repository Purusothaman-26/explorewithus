Kochi

About Kochi

Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a vibrant port city in Kerala blending colonial charm with modern culture. It’s famous for its backwaters, historic forts, and rich spice markets.

Top attractions include Fort Kochi, Chinese fishing nets, Mattancherry Palace, and the serene Vypin Island — offering a mix of heritage and seaside beauty.

Explore the Jewish Synagogue, enjoy a Kathakali performance, cruise through backwaters, or relax at Cherai Beach. Kochi is a perfect mix of culture, coast, and cuisine — ideal for both short visits and extended stays.

Top Travel Destinations in Kochi

  • Fort Kochi – Colonial streets and Chinese fishing nets
  • Mattancherry Palace – Historical Dutch palace with murals
  • Jew Town – Antique shops and Jewish Synagogue
  • Marine Drive – Scenic walkway with sea views
  • Cherai Beach – Clean beach ideal for swimming
  • Bolgatty Palace – Heritage hotel on an island
  • Hill Palace Museum – Former Maharaja's residence
  • Vypin Island – Backwaters and lighthouse beach

Travel Tips for Visiting Kochi

  • Pack light & cool clothing: Kochi is humid; cotton clothes are best.
  • Try local food: Don’t miss Kerala-style seafood and banana leaf meals.
  • Use auto/taxi apps: Kochi Metro, taxis, and auto-rickshaws are widely available.
  • Plan cultural visits: Enjoy Kathakali, Kalaripayattu shows, and festivals.
  • Protect against sun: Carry sunscreen and hats during daytime outings.
  • Respect traditions: Remove footwear in temples and dress modestly in religious areas.
  • Watch tide timings: For ferry rides or beach visits, check tides and timings.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink bottled water and carry some with you while exploring.

Kolkata

About Kolkata

Kolkata, the cultural capital of India, is located in West Bengal and is known for its colonial architecture, vibrant festivals, art, and literature. It was the former capital of British India and still holds a timeless charm.

From the majestic Howrah Bridge to the serene Dakshineswar Temple, Kolkata seamlessly blends heritage with modernity.

Discover the intellectual legacy of Rabindranath Tagore, explore the colorful local markets like New Market, savor Bengali delicacies like rasgulla and fish curry, and enjoy a boat ride on the Hooghly River. Don’t miss the tram ride — the only one in India.

Top Travel Destinations in Kolkata

  • Victoria Memorial – Marble structure symbolizing British Raj
  • Howrah Bridge – Iconic cantilever bridge over Hooghly River
  • Dakshineswar Temple – Temple dedicated to Goddess Kali
  • Indian Museum – One of the oldest museums in Asia
  • Kalighat Temple – Historic temple of Kali Mata
  • Science City – Interactive science exhibits and fun rides
  • Princep Ghat – Riverside promenade with colonial charm
  • Eco Park – Urban park with themed gardens and activities

Travel Tips for Visiting Kolkata

  • Best time to visit: October to March, especially during Durga Puja.
  • Transport: Use metro, yellow taxis, or trams for local travel.
  • Clothing: Lightweight cotton in summer, warm clothes in winter.
  • Festivals: Experience Durga Puja and Kolkata Book Fair for cultural immersion.
  • Local cuisine: Try Bengali sweets like sandesh, mishti doi, and street food.
  • Markets: Visit College Street for books and New Market for local shopping.
  • Respect: Be polite and patient in crowded public places.
  • Language: Bengali is widely spoken; English and Hindi are also understood.

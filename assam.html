Assam

About Assam

Assam, a northeastern state of India, is known for its tea gardens, Brahmaputra River, rich wildlife, and vibrant tribal culture. It’s a gateway to the northeast with breathtaking landscapes and diverse traditions.

From national parks to ancient temples and colorful festivals, Assam is a serene yet culturally dynamic destination for nature lovers and explorers alike.

Explore Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO site known for one-horned rhinos, visit the sacred Kamakhya Temple, cruise on the Brahmaputra River, and enjoy a cup of Assam tea right at the source. Assam offers unique beauty and spiritual charm.

Top Travel Destinations in Assam

  • Kaziranga National Park – Famous for one-horned rhinoceroses
  • Kamakhya Temple – Sacred temple on Nilachal Hill in Guwahati
  • Majuli Island – World’s largest river island and cultural hotspot
  • Tea Gardens of Jorhat – Tour Assam’s iconic tea estates
  • Sivasagar – Historical town of Ahom Kingdom monuments
  • Manas National Park – Tiger reserve and biosphere hotspot
  • Brahmaputra River Cruise – Scenic views and wildlife sightings
  • Haflong – The only hill station in Assam with scenic beauty

Travel Tips for Visiting Assam

  • Best season: Visit between October and April for ideal weather.
  • Pack light jackets: Nights can get cool, especially in winter.
  • Carry binoculars: Perfect for spotting birds and wildlife.
  • Respect local customs: Assam has many ethnic communities and traditions.
  • Stay eco-friendly: Protect the natural and cultural heritage.
  • Book safaris early: Kaziranga and Manas get fully booked quickly.
  • Try local cuisine: Don’t miss dishes like Khar, Masor Tenga, and Pitha.
  • Travel with ID: Especially for border areas or restricted zones.

Alleppey

About Alleppey

Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is often called the “Venice of the East” for its intricate network of backwaters, canals, and lagoons. It’s one of Kerala’s most serene and scenic destinations.

Houseboat cruises, paddy fields, and sunset views on the lake make Alleppey a peaceful retreat for nature lovers, honeymooners, and slow travelers.

Glide through the backwaters on a houseboat, experience village life, witness the famous Nehru Trophy Snake Boat Race, or enjoy Ayurvedic therapies by the water. Alleppey offers relaxation and authentic Kerala culture.

Top Travel Destinations in Alleppey

  • Alleppey Backwaters – Ideal for houseboat stays and cruises
  • Alleppey Beach – Long sandy beach with a colonial pier
  • Vembanad Lake – Kerala’s largest lake and birdwatching spot
  • Krishnapuram Palace – Heritage site with mural paintings
  • Pathiramanal Island – Scenic island in Vembanad Lake
  • Kuttanad – Known as the ‘Rice Bowl of Kerala’
  • Punnamada Lake – Famous for boat races
  • Marari Beach – Quiet beach perfect for relaxing

Travel Tips for Visiting Alleppey

  • Book houseboats early: Especially during peak season (Nov–Feb).
  • Choose A/C boats: If traveling in warmer months.
  • Carry mosquito repellent: Useful near water and evenings.
  • Try local food: Fresh fish curry and Kerala meals are must-try.
  • Respect eco-zones: Don’t litter in the backwaters or lakes.
  • Explore by bicycle: Rural Alleppey is perfect for cycling tours.
  • Stay hydrated: Coconut water is refreshing and widely available.
  • Use sunscreen: Backwater areas can get sunny even on boats.

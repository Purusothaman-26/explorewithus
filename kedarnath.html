Kedarnath

About Kedarnath

Kedarnath, nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, is one of the holiest pilgrimage sites in India. It is home to the ancient Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva and located near the Mandakini River.

Surrounded by snow-capped peaks and stunning valleys, Kedarnath combines spiritual significance with breathtaking natural beauty.

Reach Kedarnath by a 16 km trek from Gaurikund or via helicopter. Visit the temple, experience early morning aarti, explore nearby Bhairavnath Temple, and soak in the peaceful mountain air that elevates the soul.

Top Travel Destinations in Kedarnath

  • Kedarnath Temple – Sacred 8th-century Shiva temple
  • Bhairavnath Temple – Temple with views overlooking Kedarnath valley
  • Gaurikund – Hot springs and starting point for Kedarnath trek
  • Vasuki Tal – High-altitude lake surrounded by glaciers
  • Rambara – Scenic mid-point on the Kedarnath trekking route
  • Triyuginarayan Temple – Mythical marriage site of Shiva & Parvati
  • Sonprayag – Confluence of rivers with mythological relevance
  • Chorabari Tal (Gandhi Sarovar) – Glacial lake with spiritual aura

Travel Tips for Visiting Kedarnath

  • Best time to visit: May to June and September to October.
  • Clothing: Carry heavy woolens and waterproof layers.
  • Trekking: Start early and be physically prepared for high altitude.
  • Weather: Sudden weather changes are common; check forecasts.
  • Altitude: Acclimatize and stay hydrated to avoid AMS (altitude sickness).
  • Registration: Mandatory biometric registration for pilgrims.
  • Accommodation: Book ahead; limited stays near temple.
  • Respect: Maintain silence and cleanliness in temple areas.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands

About Andaman and Nicobar

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands, a group of picturesque islands in the Bay of Bengal, are known for their white-sand beaches, lush rainforests, and vibrant coral reefs. The islands are a haven for nature enthusiasts and beach lovers.

Rich in history, marine biodiversity, and tribal cultures, this tropical paradise offers the perfect mix of relaxation, adventure, and exploration.

Visit the famous Radhanagar Beach, explore the Cellular Jail’s history, dive in the coral-rich waters of Neil Island, or relax at Ross Island's colonial ruins. With clear blue seas and rich ecosystems, the islands provide an unforgettable experience.

Top Travel Destinations in Andaman and Nicobar

  • Havelock Island – Home to Radhanagar Beach and scuba diving sites
  • Neil Island – Serene beaches and coral reefs
  • Port Blair – Capital city with Cellular Jail and museums
  • Ross Island – Colonial ruins and historical landmarks
  • Baratang Island – Limestone caves and mangrove creeks
  • North Bay Island – Snorkeling and undersea walking
  • Little Andaman – Surfing and waterfall treks
  • Great Nicobar – Remote beauty and biodiversity

Travel Tips for Visiting Andaman and Nicobar

  • Best time to visit: November to April for calm seas and pleasant climate.
  • Permits: Required for some restricted Nicobar areas.
  • No foreign access: Nicobar Islands are off-limits to foreign nationals.
  • Respect tribal zones: Entry is strictly prohibited in tribal reserves.
  • Marine conservation: Avoid touching corals or littering beaches.
  • Local cuisine: Try seafood delicacies and coconut-based dishes.
  • Cash is king: Carry enough cash for remote island travel.
  • Connectivity: Mobile and internet may be patchy on some islands.

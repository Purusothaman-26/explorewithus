Ayodhya

About Ayodhya

Ayodhya, situated in Uttar Pradesh, is one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism and the birthplace of Lord Rama. It holds immense religious and cultural importance in Indian history.

With its spiritual ambience, grand temples, and mythological connections, Ayodhya attracts devotees, historians, and tourists alike.

Ayodhya is known for the newly built Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Mandir, which has become a major spiritual hub. Visitors can attend evening aartis on the ghats, witness Ramleela performances, and explore the temples and historical sites scattered across the holy town.

Top Travel Destinations in Ayodhya

  • Shri Ram Janmabhoomi Mandir – Sacred birthplace of Lord Rama
  • Hanuman Garhi – Ancient hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman
  • Kanak Bhawan – Temple gifted to Sita by Queen Kaikeyi
  • Nageshwarnath Temple – Mythological shrine by Kush, son of Rama
  • Ram Ki Paidi – Sacred bathing ghats along the Saryu River
  • Treta Ke Thakur – Temple housing idols of Rama carved from black stone
  • Guptar Ghat – Believed to be the site where Lord Rama took Jal Samadhi
  • Mani Parvat – A small hill associated with the Ramayana legend

Travel Tips for Visiting Ayodhya

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather.
  • Festivals: Plan around Ram Navami or Diwali for vibrant celebrations.
  • Clothing: Dress modestly and respect temple customs.
  • Local Transport: Use rickshaws or walking tours for exploring the town.
  • Security: Follow all temple security rules and avoid restricted zones.
  • Respect: Be quiet and respectful around temple rituals and holy places.
  • Photography: Ask before taking photos at religious sites.
  • Accommodation: Choose from dharamshalas or mid-range hotels near the ghats or temples.

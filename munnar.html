Munnar

About Munnar

Munnar is a picturesque hill station in Kerala, known for its sprawling tea gardens, misty mountains, and cool climate. It offers a peaceful escape from the heat and chaos of city life.

Nestled in the Western Ghats, Munnar is ideal for honeymooners, trekkers, and nature lovers. It's also home to rare flora and fauna, including the Neelakurinji flower that blooms once in 12 years.

Visit Eravikulam National Park to spot the Nilgiri Tahr, enjoy panoramic views from Top Station, explore tea museums, and relax by beautiful waterfalls. Munnar is a blend of scenic beauty and eco-adventure.

Top Travel Destinations in Munnar

  • Eravikulam National Park – Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr
  • Tea Museum – Learn about Munnar’s tea history and culture
  • Mattupetty Dam – Boating and scenic views of hills and water
  • Top Station – Stunning panoramic views of the Western Ghats
  • Attukal Waterfalls – Perfect for nature photography and picnics
  • Echo Point – Shout and hear your echo back!
  • Kundala Lake – Pedal boating amid green valleys
  • Blossom Park – Gardens, play area, and cycling trails

Travel Tips for Visiting Munnar

  • Carry warm clothes: Even summer nights can be chilly.
  • Travel by day: Hill roads are best driven during daylight.
  • Stay hydrated: The altitude may cause mild fatigue.
  • Try local tea: Munnar’s tea is world-famous—visit estates and shops.
  • Respect wildlife rules: Don't litter in national parks or feed animals.
  • Pre-book hotels: Especially on weekends and holidays.
  • Use offline maps: Internet signals may be weak in high areas.
  • Enjoy early mornings: Munnar is magical at sunrise with misty hills.

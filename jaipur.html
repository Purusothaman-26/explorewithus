The Pink City - Jaipur

About Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City for its trademark building color. It's famous for historical forts, palaces, and rich Rajasthani culture.

Must-visit sites include Amber Fort, Hawa Mahal, City Palace, and Jantar Mantar — all showcasing stunning architecture and royal heritage.

Jaipur is part of the Golden Triangle Tourist Circuit and offers vibrant markets, traditional crafts, and mouth-watering local cuisine. It's a UNESCO World Heritage City and a photographer’s dream!

Top Travel Places in Jaipur

  • Amber Fort: A majestic hilltop fort with beautiful architecture and elephant rides.
  • Hawa Mahal: The iconic "Palace of Winds" with 953 small windows.
  • City Palace: A royal residence with museums, courtyards, and stunning designs.
  • Jantar Mantar: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with ancient astronomical instruments.
  • Jal Mahal: A palace floating in the middle of Man Sagar Lake.
  • Albert Hall Museum: Jaipur’s oldest museum featuring art, crafts, and royal artifacts.
  • Nahargarh Fort: Offers panoramic views of Jaipur, especially at sunset.
  • Birla Mandir: A modern white-marble temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi.

Travel Tips for Visiting Jaipur

  • Best Time to Visit: October to March offers pleasant weather for sightseeing.
  • Wear Comfortable Footwear: You’ll do a lot of walking around forts and markets.
  • Stay Hydrated: Jaipur can get hot, especially during the day—carry a water bottle.
  • Respect Local Culture: Dress modestly when visiting temples and royal sites.
  • Bargain in Markets: Local bazaars are great for souvenirs, but always negotiate prices.
  • Try Local Food: Don’t miss dishes like Dal Baati Churma, Ghewar, and Laal Maas.
  • Book Tickets Online: For popular spots like City Palace or Amber Fort, book early to skip the line.
  • Use Local Transport: Use auto-rickshaws or book Ola/Uber for easy travel inside the city.

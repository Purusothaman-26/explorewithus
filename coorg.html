Coorg

About Coorg

Coorg, also known as Kodagu, is a beautiful hill station in Karnataka famous for its coffee plantations, misty hills, and lush greenery. It’s a serene destination ideal for nature lovers and peace seekers.

With its cool climate, rich culture, and scenic beauty, Coorg is often called the "Scotland of India."

Explore the tranquil Abbey Falls, enjoy a walk through aromatic coffee estates, trek to Tadiandamol Peak, or visit the Dubare Elephant Camp. Coorg offers a peaceful retreat with a blend of adventure and cultural experiences.

Top Travel Destinations in Coorg

  • Abbey Falls – Picturesque waterfall surrounded by spice plantations
  • Dubare Elephant Camp – Interaction and bathing sessions with elephants
  • Raja’s Seat – Sunset point with panoramic valley views
  • Tadiandamol Peak – Highest peak in Coorg, great for trekking
  • Madikeri Fort – Historical site with colonial-era charm
  • Namdroling Monastery – Golden Temple showcasing Tibetan architecture
  • Cauvery Nisargadhama – Island park with bamboo groves and deer park
  • Omkareshwara Temple – Unique blend of Islamic and Gothic styles

Travel Tips for Visiting Coorg

  • Best time to visit: October to March for cool weather and clear views.
  • Local transport: Cabs or rented bikes are best for local travel.
  • Clothing: Carry light woolens and comfortable shoes for trekking.
  • Connectivity: Limited mobile signal in remote areas; download offline maps.
  • Wildlife safety: Avoid wandering into forest trails without guides.
  • Cash: Carry cash as some areas have limited ATM access.
  • Local food: Try Coorg pork curry, bamboo shoot dishes, and homemade chocolates.
  • Respect plantations: Do not pluck coffee or disturb private estates.

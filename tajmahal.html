The Taj Mahal

About Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a symbol of love and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it's located in Agra, India.

The stunning white marble structure took over 20 years to complete and showcases beautiful Mughal architecture, intricate carvings, and a peaceful garden layout.

Completed in 1653, the Taj Mahal includes a mosque, guest house, and reflection pool. It draws millions of visitors every year and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Travel Tips for Visiting the Taj Mahal

  • Visit Early Morning: Arrive at sunrise to avoid crowds and capture beautiful photos.
  • No Large Bags: Security is strict—avoid bringing large bags or tripods.
  • Buy Tickets Online: Skip the line by booking tickets in advance via the official website.
  • Wear Shoe Covers: You must wear shoe covers (provided) or go barefoot when entering the mausoleum.
  • Closed on Fridays: The Taj Mahal is closed to tourists every Friday for prayers.
  • Hire a Guide: Consider a licensed guide for historical insights and interesting facts.
  • Respect the Rules: No food, drones, or photography inside the main mausoleum.
  • Hydrate and Dress Comfortably: Summers in Agra are hot—carry water and wear light clothes.

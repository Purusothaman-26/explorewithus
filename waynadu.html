Wayanad

About Wayanad

Wayanad, located in Kerala, is a scenic district known for its rich forests, mist-covered hills, and wildlife sanctuaries. It's a nature lover’s paradise with waterfalls, caves, plantations, and trekking trails.

A perfect mix of adventure and tranquility, Wayanad offers beautiful views, tribal heritage, and eco-tourism experiences in the lap of the Western Ghats.

Visit Edakkal Caves with ancient carvings, trek to Chembra Peak for a heart-shaped lake, enjoy bamboo rafting at Kuruva Island, and explore the rich biodiversity at Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary. Ideal for families, couples, and nature seekers.

Top Travel Destinations in Wayanad

  • Chembra Peak – Trek to the famous heart-shaped lake
  • Banasura Sagar Dam – Scenic picnic spot and boating
  • Edakkal Caves – Ancient petroglyphs and history
  • Soochipara Falls – Majestic three-tiered waterfall
  • Kuruva Island – Bamboo rafting and serene forest vibes
  • Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary – Spot elephants, deer, and birds
  • Meenmutty Falls – Trek through forest to reach these falls
  • Pookode Lake – Freshwater lake with pedal boats and aquariums

Travel Tips for Visiting Wayanad

  • Visit post-monsoon: The greenery and waterfalls are at their best.
  • Wear trekking shoes: Many places involve hikes and walks.
  • Carry insect repellent: Especially for forest areas.
  • Stay in eco-resorts: For an immersive nature experience.
  • Respect local tribes: Many areas are culturally sensitive zones.
  • Check permissions: Some treks require forest department passes.
  • Try tribal cuisine: Available in homestays and local markets.
  • Stay hydrated: Especially during treks in humid conditions.
